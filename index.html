The Benefits of Fresh Vegetables

Fresh vegetables are a great source of essential nutrients. From leafy greens to root vegetables, adding them to your daily diet can help lower blood pressure, reduce the risk of heart disease, and improve digestion.

How to Choose the Best Seasonal Produce

Seasonal produce is more likely to be at its peak freshness, flavor, and nutritional value. Some of the best seasonal choices include berries in summer, root vegetables in autumn, and citrus fruits in winter.

Healthy Cooking Tips for a Balanced Diet

Frequently Asked Questions

How to keep fruits and vegetables fresh?

To keep fruits and vegetables fresh, store them in cool, dry places. Refrigerate items like berries, leafy greens, & herbs in airtight containers.
For root vegetables like potatoes and onions, keep them in a dark, cool spot outside the fridge.
Washing produce before storing can speed up spoilage, so it’s best to wash them just before use.

Which is healthier. a fruit or a vegetable? Why?

Both fruits and vegetables are healthy, but their benefits differ slightly.
Fruits are rich in natural sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants, while vegetables are typically lower in sugar and higher in fiber, vitamins, & minerals.
Both offer essential nutrients, but vegetables tend to have fewer calories & can be more nutrient-dense, making them slightly healthier for maintaining a balanced diet.
